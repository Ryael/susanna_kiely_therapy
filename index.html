Person-Centered Therapy

Person-centered therapy is a therapeutic approach that focuses on the client's ability to heal and grow in a supportive, non-judgmental environment.

Developed by Carl Rogers, it emphasizes empathy, acceptance, and authenticity from the therapist, allowing clients to explore their emotions and find their own solutions.

Instead of giving advice, the therapist acts as a compassionate guide, helping clients tap into their inner strengths to foster personal growth and self-awareness.

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy

Cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) is based on the concept that your thoughts, feelings, physical sensations and actions are interconnected, and that negative thoughts and feelings can trap you in a negative cycle.

CBT aims to help you deal with overwhelming problems in a more positive way by breaking them down into smaller parts.

The therapy teaches new effective coping strategies to help you deal better with difficulties even after your therapy has finished.

Psychodynamic Therapy

Psychodynamic therapy is a form of talk therapy that focuses on exploring unconscious thoughts, emotions, and past experiences, particularly those from childhood, to understand current behaviors and feelings.

It aims to help individuals gain insight into unresolved conflicts and repressed emotions, leading to better self-awareness and emotional healing.

The therapy often involves exploring patterns in relationships, defence mechanisms, and recurring themes in the person’s life.
